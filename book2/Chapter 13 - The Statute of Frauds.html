The Statute of Frauds

Some contracts must be in writing to be enforceable — verbal alone won't do.
Writing requiredInterests in landGuaranteesUnenforceable ≠ void

What Must Be in Writing

Interests in land

Any contract for the sale of land or an interest in it (incl. ownership and leasehold interests) must be in writing to be enforceable.

Guarantees

A contract of guarantee (promising to answer for another's debt) must be in writing to be enforceable.

Two Important Nuances

Unenforceable, not void

A verbal contract caught by the Statute isn't void — courts still recognize it exists for certain purposes; it just can't be enforced by action.

Why writing matters anyway

Even where not required, verbal terms are hard to prove. Written contracts avoid disputes over what was actually agreed.

Engineer's takeaway: put it in writing — especially anything touching land or a guarantee. A statute of frauds exists in every common-law province.
