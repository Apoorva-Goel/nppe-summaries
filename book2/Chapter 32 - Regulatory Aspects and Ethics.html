Why Regulation Exists

Principal object

Each province's statute (e.g. Professional Engineers Act) exists to regulate the practice and protect the public interest — not to protect engineers.

Self-regulation

The Association governs its members, sets entry standards, licenses, and disciplines — a privilege conditional on protecting the public.

Additional objects

Establish/maintain standards of knowledge, skill, qualification, and ethical practice among members.

"Practice of Professional Engineering"

Defined in each statute as any act of planning, designing, composing, evaluating, advising, reporting, directing or supervising that requires application of engineering principles and concerns the safeguarding of life, health, property, economic interests, public welfare or the environment.

The Engineer's Seal

When to seal

In all common-law provinces, a P.Eng. must stamp drawings and specifications with their seal when providing engineering services to the public — signing & dating it.

Seal = responsibility

Sealing certifies the engineer took responsibility for the work. Sealing a document you didn't prepare or supervise is professional misconduct.

NPPE focus: the regulator's mandate is always public protection. Know the scope of "practice of engineering," when sealing is required, and that improper use of the seal (rubber-stamping) is a defined act of professional misconduct.
