The Duty of Honesty

Three Criminal Exposures

Fraud (s.380)

Defrauding the public or any person by deceit, falsehood or other fraudulent means is an indictable offence — up to 10 years imprisonment.

Secret commissions (s.426)

An agent who corruptly accepts a reward/benefit relating to the principal's affairs — or anyone who offers one — commits an offence. Convictions carry imprisonment.

Government gifts (s.121)

It's an offence for a government employee to accept a gift from someone dealing with the government — extra caution on public projects.

Why the Engineer Is Exposed

The engineer is normally retained as the client's agent. That fiduciary position demands undivided loyalty: no kickbacks, no undisclosed interests, no false documents intended to mislead the principal. A "receipt, account or other writing" that is false or misleading triggers liability under the secret-commissions provisions.

Bottom line: the duty of honesty isn't just professional ethics — breaches can be criminal offences carrying prison time. Disclose conflicts; never accept secret benefits.
