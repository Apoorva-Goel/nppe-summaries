The Competition Act

Federal law protecting competition — with criminal offences engineers and their associations can stumble into.

The Core Prohibitions — mindmap

Conspiracy (s.45)

  • Agreements among competitors to:
  • fix prices
  • allocate markets/customers
  • restrict supply
  • Criminal offence

Bid-rigging

  • Separate criminal offence
  • Coordinating or withholding bids
  • Direct hit to tendering

Abuse of dominance (s.79)

  • Anti-competitive conduct by a dominant firm
  • Reviewable (civil)

Defences & Nuances

Ancillary agreements defence

Restrictions reasonably necessary to a broader legitimate agreement — e.g. a non-compete in a sale of business — may be permissible.

Reach beyond Canada

Under s.46, a corporation in Canada implementing a conspiracy formed abroad can still be liable.

Trade / Professional Associations — danger zone

Industry & professional association meetings are prime settings for accidental conspiracy. Safeguards: compliance programs; aggregate sensitive data through a third party; agendas & accurate minutes; if improper talk starts (prices, market/customer allocation), leave and have your departure noted; don't coerce members for data; don't misuse standard-setting to disadvantage rivals.

Engineer's takeaway: never discuss prices, bids, or dividing up customers/markets with competitors — even casually at an association event. The penalties are criminal.
