Mistake

When a contract is recorded wrong, or one side bids in error — what relief is available?
RectificationCommon mistakeUnilateral mistakeRon Engineering

Types of Mistake

Common mistake → Rectification

Parties clearly agreed, but the written contract records it inaccurately. The court can rectify (correct) the document to reflect the true agreement.

Unilateral mistake

Only one party is mistaken. Courts are reluctant to grant rescission — doing so would "destroy stability and certainty" in contracting (Imperial Glass v Consolidated Supplies, BCCA 1960).

The Tendering Mistake — Ron Engineering (SCC 1981)

A contractor submitted a tender containing a clerical error and tried to withdraw its bid deposit. The Supreme Court held the contractor was contractually bound to forfeit the deposit — because submitting the tender created a separate contract ("Contract A") governed by the tender terms, including the forfeiture provision.

The big idea: the bid itself forms Contract A the moment it's submitted — a binding tendering contract, distinct from the eventual construction contract (Contract B). This launched modern tendering law → see Ch 16.
