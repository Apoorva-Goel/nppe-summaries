Discoverability — when the 2-year clock starts

The claimant knew (or a reasonable person ought to have known) that: injury/loss occurred, it was caused by the defendant's act or omission, the defendant was involved, and that a proceeding would be an appropriate remedy. The clock is also paused during willful concealment.

Statutory Notes

2002 Act

In force Jan 1, 2004 — sweeping reform; originally barred contracting out of the statutory periods.

2006 amendment

Access to Justice Act, 2006 — for business agreements after Oct 16, 2006, parties may vary the periods.

Other provinces

Periods and ultimate limits vary; courts elsewhere have endorsed the discoverability concept.

Engineer's takeaway: design/inspection liability can surface years later. The 15-year ultimate limit is a critical backstop — diarize key dates and don't rely on memory of when work was done.
