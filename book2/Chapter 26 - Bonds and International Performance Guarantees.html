The Three Parties to a Bond

Surety

The bonding company that guarantees performance.

Principal

The party whose obligation is guaranteed (usually the contractor).

Obligee

The party protected by the bond (usually the owner).

A bond is NOT insurance. If the surety pays out, it can pursue the principal for reimbursement — unlike insurance, the contractor remains on the hook.

Types of Bonds & Security

Bid bond

Used in tendering — guarantees the bidder will enter the contract if selected, or forfeit.

Performance bond

Indemnifies the obligee if the principal fails to perform. Amount covers the extra cost above contract price to complete. Clearest trigger: contractor bankrupt / receiver appointed. Read the wording carefully.

Labour & material payment bond

Guarantees that subcontractors & suppliers get paid for labour and materials.

Letters of credit / demand bonds

Can act as a "blank cheque" — payable on demand. Common abroad (Middle East, Africa, SE Asia) as "performance bonds." Get legal advice; beware "bad calls."
