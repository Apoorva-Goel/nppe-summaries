Fundamental Breach — a retired doctrine

Old rule (Harbutt's Plasticine)

A breach going to "the very root" of the contract automatically stripped away any limitation clause. Applied in Canada ~1970–1980.

Now (Photo Production → Tercon)

The doctrine is gone. Whether a limitation clause survives a serious breach is decided by interpretation + the Tercon test, not an automatic rule.

Tercon facts: BC issued an RFP to prequalified bidders; an exclusion clause barred claims. The Province effectively accepted an ineligible bidder. The SCC held the clause didn't cover that conduct → Tercon recovered. Lesson: exclusion clauses are read narrowly and won't shield a party from its own improper process.
