A Different Legal Foundation

Civil Code of Quebec

The cornerstone of Quebec's civil-law system — governs persons, property, and relations between persons. Not based on common-law precedent.

Ordre des ingénieurs du Québec

The Engineers Act of Quebec defines the exclusive field of practice. OIQ members must carry mandatory professional liability insurance.

Liability Under the Civil Code

Contractual liability

Arises from breach of a contractual obligation.

Extracontractual liability

The civil-law equivalent of tort — every person has a duty to abide by the rules of conduct applicable in the circumstances; breach causing injury creates liability.

Consumer & Adhesion Contracts

The Code protects the weaker party in a consumer contract or contract of adhesion: an external clause may be void if not brought to the party's attention; an illegible/incomprehensible clause may be void; and an excessive or unreasonably detrimental (abusive) clause may be reduced or struck.

Engineer's takeaway: never assume common-law rules apply in Quebec. Retain a Quebec lawyer, understand contractual vs extracontractual liability, and ensure OIQ membership + PLI before practising there.
