Risks in Construction

Construction is a high-risk industry — the project structure decides who carries which risk.
Risk allocationProject structuresDesign-build / BOT / P3CCDC forms

Project Delivery Structures — mindmap

Traditional

  • Owner → designer + general contractor
  • Design separate from build
  • Familiar risk split

Construction Mgmt (multi-prime)

  • Owner holds many prime contracts
  • CM coordinates
  • Owner keeps more risk

Design-Build

  • One entity designs & builds
  • Single point of responsibility

BOT / P3

  • Build-Operate-Transfer; public/private partnership
  • Contractor takes on operational risk

Risk Allocation Principles

Project-by-project

Risk assessment & allocation must be done for each project on its facts — no one-size template.

Risk has a price

When risk shifts to a party, that party will price it in. Dumping risk on the contractor inflates the bid.

Use CCDC forms

CCDC standard-form contracts (owner–contractor) balance both sides' interests — but review them carefully every time.

Engineer's takeaway: the smartest risk is allocated to the party best able to control or absorb it. International projects add political risk; P3/BOT add operational risk the traditional contractor never carried.
