Two Styles of Ground-Conditions Clause

Owner-favourable

Contractor acknowledges it inspected the site and relies on its own investigation; owner's reports don't limit the contractor's obligations. Shifts all subsurface risk to the contractor.

Contractor-favourable ("changed conditions")

If actual conditions substantially differ from the information provided, the contractor may claim extra payment — provided it gives written notice (e.g. within 10 days) and the engineer agrees the claim is justified.

Key Practice Points

Risk has a cost

Shifting all subsurface risk to the contractor invites inflated bids (or disputes when conditions surprise).

Investigate first

Prior subsurface investigation by qualified experts is strongly advisable for both sides.

Notice is decisive

Contractors have lost claims for failing to follow the contract's notice/disclaimer terms — comply strictly.

Engineer's takeaway: the differing-site-conditions clause and its notice provisions decide these disputes. Encourage realistic geotechnical investigation and document everything — courts often deny contractor claims where the contract warned of variable conditions.
