Mutual Intent

A contract document should specify the parties' agreement on all essential terms. If essential terms are missing or left "to be agreed later," a binding contract may not yet exist.

The Letter of Intent Trap

The danger

A "letter of intent" is common — but whether it's binding depends on substance, not the label. If it contains all essential terms, it may be a full contract even if a "further agreement" was contemplated.

The test

If the document is complete in itself, it binds. If essential terms are missing and a future contract is genuinely required, it does not. Courts look past the name.

Engineer's takeaway: never assume a "letter of intent" is non-binding — or that it is. State expressly whether it is binding, and pin down every essential term before you rely on it.
