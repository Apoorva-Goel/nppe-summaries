Leading Causes of Construction Disputes — mindmap

Risk-shifting

  • Aggressive contracting that unreasonably shifts risk
  • Aggressive extra claims in response

Documents

  • Ambiguities in contract documents
  • Fast-track = incomplete design

Money & coordination

  • Insufficient financing
  • Poor contractor management & coordination

People

  • Poor communication
  • Litigious mind-sets
  • No team spirit
  • Indecisive administrators

The Trend & Response

A global movement

The "ADR revolution" began in the U.S.; Canada has trailed but is catching up. ADR is now central to international construction conferences.

Fast-track pressure

Building before design is finished creates fertile ground for disputes — making a built-in ADR system even more important.

Engineer's takeaway: most disputes trace back to unfair risk allocation, unclear documents, and poor communication. Designing an ADR process into the contract up front is far cheaper than litigating later — especially abroad.
