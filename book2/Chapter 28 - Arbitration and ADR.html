The ADR Spectrum — binding ↔ non-binding

Litigation

  • Court process
  • Binding adjudication
  • Public, slow, costly

Arbitration

  • Private adjudicator
  • Binding award
  • Governed by Arbitration Act

Mediation

  • Neutral facilitator
  • Non-binding
  • Aims at negotiated settlement

Partnering / Dispute Boards

  • Prevent & resolve early
  • Better attitudes & communication
Key distinction: only litigation and arbitration produce a binding adjudication. Mediation/conciliation only works if the parties agree to settle.

The Arbitration Act

Limited court interference

Courts may only set aside an award in narrow circumstances — so arbitration isn't just a "prologue" to a later lawsuit.

Independent arbitrator

Codifies the common-law rule: the arbitrator must be independent and impartial.

Expanded powers

The Act broadens the powers arbitrators can exercise to run an effective process.

Preventive ADR

Partnering

A post-award workshop to align attitudes among project participants and head off disputes.

Dispute resolution boards / neutrals

Standing panels that resolve issues as they arise during the project, before they escalate.
