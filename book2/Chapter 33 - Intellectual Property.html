Patent Requirements

Invention

"Any new and useful art, process, machine, manufacture or composition of matter" — or a new & useful improvement. A mere idea isn't enough; it must be reduced to a definite, practical form.

Novelty & non-obviousness

Must not be anticipated (already known) and must not be obvious — judged against the state of the art at the claim date.

Infringement

The owner has the exclusive right to use/license. Remedies include an injunction and damages.

Employee Engineer's Inventions

Generally the inventor is entitled to apply for the patent. But absent a special contract, an invention made in the employer's time, with the employer's materials, at the employer's expense may belong to the employer — especially where the employee was hired to invent (British Reinforced Concrete v Lind). Employment/IP-assignment agreements usually settle this expressly.

Engineer's takeaway: assignment of patent rights must be in writing. Check your employment contract — it likely assigns inventions to your employer. Keep records of conception dates.
