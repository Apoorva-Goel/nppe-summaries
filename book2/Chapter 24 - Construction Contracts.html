As CERTIFIER / quasi-arbitratorCertifying payment & deciding disputes — must act judicially & impartially
Certificates are binding on the parties in the absence of fraud or bad faith (Kamlee v Oakville; Croft Construction). When certifying, the engineer must act fairly between owner and contractor — not simply favour the paying owner.

Inspection vs Supervision — Demers v Dufresne (SCC 1977)

Engineer = inspector

The engineer normally inspects the result — it does not supervise or dictate the contractor's work methods.

Means & methods = contractor's

In Demers, a caisson exploded due to a glaring error in the contractor's chosen method — that risk lay with the contractor, not the inspecting engineer.

Engineer's takeaway: certify honestly and impartially, document inspections, and don't take on responsibility for the contractor's construction means and methods unless the contract clearly requires it.
