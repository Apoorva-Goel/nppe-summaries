Keeping an Offer Open

Irrevocable offer

Offeror expressly binds itself not to revoke. The offeree gains certainty the offer will stand.

Option contract

A separate contract (with its own consideration) to hold an offer open for a set time. Common in mining and real-estate deals.

Timing Rules & the "Mailbox" Doctrine

Acceptance

Under the postal/mailbox rule, acceptance can be effective when sent. (Courts limit it — see Commercial Credit, NS 1983, where the doctrine did not apply.)

Revocation

Different rule: revocation is generally only effective when received by the offeree. Governing law is set by where the contract is made.

Battle of the forms: suppliers attach their own terms to quotations and acknowledgements. Acceptance of an offer (or counter-offer) can bind you to terms you didn't intend. Examine and negotiate the fine print — and for cross-border goods, check the UN Convention (CISG).
