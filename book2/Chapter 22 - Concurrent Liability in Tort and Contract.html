Concurrent Liability in Tort & Contract

One careless act can expose the engineer to claims in both contract and tort at the same time.
Same standard of careConcurrent liabilityNegligence ActBG Checo

The Core Principle

Same standard, two routes

Unless the contract says otherwise, the standard of care owed under a contract is the same as in tort. A plaintiff may sue in both.

Both can apply

Dominion Chain (Ont CA 1976): an engineer or contractor can be concurrently liable in tort as well as contract. Confirmed by SCC in BG Checo (1993).

Why It Matters

Limitation periods

Tort & contract clocks can start at different times — a plaintiff may use whichever still lives.

Apportioning fault

The Negligence Act lets courts split liability among concurrent tortfeasors by their degree of fault.

Contract can limit it

Parties can contractually exclude or cap the tort duty — but only with clear wording.

Engineer's takeaway: a disclaimer in your contract doesn't automatically kill a tort claim. To limit concurrent tort liability you must say so expressly and clearly in the agreement.
