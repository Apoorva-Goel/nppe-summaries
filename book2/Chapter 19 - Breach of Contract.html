Condition vs Warranty — does the breach end the contract?

Condition (fundamental term)

Goes to the root of the contract. Breach lets the innocent party terminate AND claim damages.

Warranty (minor term)

A lesser term. Breach gives a right to damages only — the contract continues.

Repudiation: if a party expressly states it will not perform, that's repudiation — the innocent party may treat the contract as ended and sue. Note: every breach allows a damages action, but not every breach discharges the contract.

Damages — Hadley v Baxendale

The rule

Recoverable damages must flow naturally from the breach OR have been reasonably foreseeable by both parties when the contract was made.

Direct vs indirect

Direct: flow naturally. Indirect/consequential: e.g. lost profits — recoverable only if special circumstances were communicated up front.

Quantum meruit

Where services were requested & performed but no price agreed, recovery is "as much as is deserved" — a reasonable value.

Two Limits on Recovery

Duty to mitigate

The injured party must take reasonable steps to reduce its loss — it can't sit back and let damages pile up.

Penalty vs liquidated damages

A pre-set sum that's a genuine pre-estimate of loss is enforceable (liquidated damages). One designed to punish is an unenforceable penalty.
