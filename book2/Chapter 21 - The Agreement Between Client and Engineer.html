The Agreement Between Client & Engineer

The retainer that governs the engineer's own work — and the agency duties that come with it.

Key Features of the Retainer

Agency relationship

The engineer usually acts as agent; the client is the principal. This imposes fiduciary-style duties of loyalty, care & honesty.

Put it in writing

The client-engineer agreement should be written and contain all essential elements (scope, fee, responsibilities).

Fee estimates

Estimate the total fee cautiously — a casually low estimate can become a ceiling on what the engineer can charge and a measure of liability.

Standards & Statutes to Know

Use standard forms

Recommended forms of agreement (client–engineer and engineer–consultant) are published by professional associations — use them rather than ad-hoc wording.

Many statutes apply

The engineer must comply with a long list of relevant statutes, e.g. Professional Engineers Act, Building Code Act, Construction Act, Occupational Health & Safety Act, Environmental Protection Act, Planning Act, and more.

Remember: fundamental breach is retired (Ch 20). The engineer is still expected to meet ordinary tort and contract standards of care — a written retainer with a clear scope is the best protection.
