Owner's Obligations Under Contract A

Accept only compliant bids

A compliant bid meets all terms/conditions of the tender. Accepting a non-compliant bid breaches Contract A with the other bidders.

Treat all bidders fairly

Implied duty of fairness & equal treatment (Chinook Aggregates, BCCA 1989 — secret local preference breached this).

Implied terms

Like all contracts, Contract A can carry implied obligations beyond the express words (Martel Building v Canada, SCC 2000).

Privilege clause: a clause stating "the lowest or any bid need not be accepted" gives the owner discretion — but it does not let the owner accept a non-compliant bid or treat bidders unfairly. The owner may also reserve the right to reject all bids.

Subcontractor Bids & Engineer Exposure

Sub-bids

"Last-minute" telephone sub-bids are common and can also create binding obligations (e.g. Gloge Heating & Plumbing). Bid protests are now frequent.

Engineers / consultants

Engineers administering tenders can face liability for mishandling the process. Get legal advice when doubts arise.
