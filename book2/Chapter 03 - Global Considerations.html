Global Considerations

Working abroad opens opportunity — and a new set of risks the Canadian engineer must manage.
Foreign marketsPolitical riskForeign legal systemsLocal advice

Risk Landscape Abroad — mindmap

Opportunity

  • Privatization & infrastructure gaps
  • Developing + developed markets
  • New foreign markets

Political Risk

  • Instability, expropriation
  • Most obvious in developing states
  • Insurance via World Bank / funding agencies

Legal Systems

  • Rights enforced differently
  • Property/mortgage security may not exist
  • Courts work differently

Business Form

  • Entity choice matters abroad too
  • Different rules per country

Managing the Risk

Get local advice

Retain an experienced lawyer in the foreign jurisdiction, or a consultant familiar with it. Don't assume Canadian rules apply.

Commit real resources

e.g. in Southeast Asia, assign carefully selected senior personnel and schedule a realistic — often longer — time commitment.

Map the contracts

Complex projects stack many agreements: supply, transportation, design, construction — each must hold up under local law.

Core lesson: opportunity abroad is real, but enforceability of rights, property security, and the court system can all differ fundamentally. Local expertise is non-negotiable.
