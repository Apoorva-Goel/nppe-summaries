Frustration — the high bar

Frustration applies only when an unforeseen event makes performance radically different from what was agreed — not mere hardship, inconvenience, or extra cost. Classic illustration: Davis Contractors v Fareham; followed in Swanson Construction v Manitoba (Man CA 1963). A contractor who simply finds the job harder/more expensive is not excused.

Engineer's takeaway: don't rely on frustration to escape a tough job. Protect yourself in advance with a clear termination / force-majeure clause (discharge by express terms).
