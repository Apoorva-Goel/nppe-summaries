What a Construction Lien Does

The purpose

Anyone supplying services or materials to an "improvement" can claim a lien against the land — security for getting paid. Rights exist in addition to ordinary contract rights.

"Improvement" (broad)

Any alteration, addition or capital repair; any construction/erection/installation; or complete/partial demolition or removal on the land.

Ontario's Construction Act — Key Reforms

Modernized (2018)

Renamed from the Construction Lien Act; transition provisions decide which rules apply to which project.

Prompt payment

Mandatory payment timelines (inspired by UK law, also adopted in Australia, NZ, etc.).

Statutory adjudication

Fast interim dispute resolution to keep cash flowing during the project.

Holdback: the Act requires mandatory release of holdback to contractors and subcontractors (unless properly disputed) — owners can no longer sit on it indefinitely.
Engineer's takeaway: lien rights are strictly time-limited — liens must be preserved and perfected within prescribed periods or they vanish. Always check the specific provincial statute for the deadlines; every province differs.
