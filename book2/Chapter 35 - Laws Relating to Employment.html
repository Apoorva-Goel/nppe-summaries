Federal vs Provincial — who governs whom

Federal — Canada Labour Code

Governs federally-regulated industries. Plus national programs: CPP (pension deductions), EI (premiums), and the Employment Equity Act (federal employers/Crown corps ≥100 employees file annual reports).

Provincial — Employment Standards Act

Governs most employees. Sets minimum terms (hours, wages, leaves). On a sale of business, retained employees keep continuous service.

Termination — Two Layers

Statutory minimum

The ESA sets a floor for notice/termination pay. In Ontario, employers with payroll ≥$2.5M owe severance pay to employees with 5+ years' service.

Common-law reasonable notice

Usually greater than the statutory minimum — assessed case-by-case on position, length of service, age, and re-employment prospects.

Workers' Compensation

Tort replacement

Replaces the right to sue the employer for workplace injury.

No-fault

Benefits paid regardless of who was at fault.

Employer-funded

Employers pay premiums into the accident fund.

Benefits

Income replacement (main), impairment, survivor, health care, rehab.

Engineer/manager takeaway: the ESA is only a floor — wrongful dismissal claims turn on common-law reasonable notice, which is often longer. Workers' comp is a no-fault bargain: guaranteed benefits in exchange for giving up the right to sue.
