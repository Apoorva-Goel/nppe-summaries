Tort Liability

The law of negligence — the single biggest source of professional liability for engineers.
Duty of careStandard of careCausationForeseeabilityProfessional liability insurance

The 3-Part Negligence Formula — all three required

1 · Duty of CareDefendant owed plaintiff a duty to use reasonable care
2 · BreachConduct fell below the required standard of care
3 · CausationThat breach caused the plaintiff's injury/damage
▼ all three proven, on a balance of probabilities ▼
Liability in tort — plaintiff is compensated
Miss any one element → the claim fails. Reasonableness runs through all three, especially foreseeability: would a reasonable person have foreseen that careless conduct would likely cause this harm?

Key Concepts

Standard of care

A professional must exercise the skill & care of a reasonably competent engineer in the field — not perfection, but the accepted standard.

Purpose of tort law

To compensate victims. So engineers serving the public should carry adequate cover so funds exist to pay.

No contract needed

Even gratuitous services (no contract) can create tort liability if performed negligently.

Insuring the Risk

Professional liability insurance

Protects the engineer where negligence causes loss in tort. The standard mechanism for ensuring compensation funds are available.

Self-insurance

Some companies / Crown agencies self-insure — acceptable if adequate funds are set aside. Note: not all engineering risks are insurable.
