Who Has Capacity?

Minors

Contracts with minors are generally not enforceable against the minor (protective rule) — except contracts for necessaries. The minor may often still enforce against the adult.

Mental incompetence / intoxication

Contracts for non-necessaries are unenforceable against the incapacitated party if the other party knew (or ought to have known) of the incapacity.

Corporations

Exercise caution: confirm the corporation has authority and the signatory has the power to bind it. Verify the entity exists and is in good standing.

Engineer's takeaway: before signing, confirm the other side actually has capacity and authority to bind — especially the right corporate signing officer.
