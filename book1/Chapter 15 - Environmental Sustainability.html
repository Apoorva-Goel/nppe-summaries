What Sustainability Means

Brundtland definition: development that "meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs." Simple to state — but profound for society. Unsustainable lifestyles threaten future security.

Climate change

CO₂ and other greenhouse gases from combustion cause global warming.

Peak oil

Demand for finite oil & gas approaches the limits of production.

From Emission to Climate Change

Combustion of coal, oil & gas → waste gases (mainly CO₂) → accumulate in the atmosphere → trap heat (greenhouse effect) → global warming & climate change. The IPCC reports synthesize the science; the Montreal Protocol (ozone) shows coordinated action can work.

Engineer's takeaway: sustainability is now a core professional responsibility — design for resource efficiency, lifecycle impact, and emissions reduction, not just immediate function.
