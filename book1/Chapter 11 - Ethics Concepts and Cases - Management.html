Creating a Professional Workplace

Professionals must be licensed

Every province restricts the practice of engineering/geoscience to licensed people — a manager must ensure staff doing reserved work are licensed.

Titles must be accurate

Job titles using "engineer"/"geoscientist" (e.g. "Project Engineer") must reflect actual licensure — misuse misleads the public and breaches the Act.

Hiring & Employment

Best practice: clear employment contracts specifying duties, duration (fixed or indefinite), and terms. Large firms may use standard policies rather than individual contracts. Managers must know provincial hiring/dismissal regulations.

Just Cause for Dismissal — termination without notice/severance

Misconduct

Serious misconduct; theft, fraud or dishonesty; willful disobedience of a lawful order.

Neglect & incompetence

Habitual neglect of duty; serious incompetence (not mere dissatisfaction); inadequate performance over time.

Conduct & attendance

Continual insolence/insubordination; excessive absenteeism despite counselling; conduct prejudicial to the business.

Manager's takeaway: just-cause dismissal (no notice/severance) requires serious, substantiated grounds — and cause discovered after discharge can still justify it. Use clear contracts, accurate titles, and licensed staff to keep the workplace lawful and ethical.
