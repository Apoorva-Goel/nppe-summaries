Achieving Diversity & Equity

The benefit

A diverse workforce strengthens the professions. Society's attitudes to fairness & equity evolve over time.

The barrier

Unfair practices persist because they are subtle or systemic — or because people are unaware of their effects.

Discrimination & the Charter

s.15(1) — Equality rights

"Every individual is equal before and under the law" without discrimination. The core protection professionals must uphold.

s.7 — Life, liberty, security

Guarantees the right to life, liberty & security of the person.

Why it binds professionals: all contracts, collective agreements, work protocols and handbooks must conform to the Charter. Discriminatory policies are unlawful — and unethical.

Underrepresentation — the data

Undergrad

Women are a majority of university undergrads overall, yet a minority in engineering & geoscience (higher in environmental, geological, biosystems).

Postgrad

~20% of full-time engineering master's students were women.

Faculty

Women rose from 2.2% (1991) to 7.6% (2001) of engineering faculty — progress, but still low.
