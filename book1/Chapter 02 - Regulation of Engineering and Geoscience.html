Three Functions of a Regulator

1 · Admit the qualified

License only people who meet the required education & experience standards.

2 · Set standards

Establish standards of practice and a Code of Ethics.

3 · Discipline

Discipline the negligent, incompetent, or unscrupulous (see Ch 3).

Why self-regulation matters: the profession governs itself in the public interest. In some countries engineering isn't licensed and anyone may call themselves an "engineer" — Canada protects both title and practice.

Evolution of Licensing

Engineering

Efforts began 1887 (CSCE). The US enacted the first law (1907). The CSCE → EIC (1918) drafted a Model Act; provinces licensed engineers through the early 20th century.

Geoscience

Initially licensed as engineers (often mining engineers). Following Alberta's lead, now licensed in most provinces by joint Associations of engineers & geoscientists.

The "Qualified Person" (QP)

After the Bre-X mining fraud and the Walkerton water tragedy, regulators introduced the Qualified Person: an engineer or geoscientist with a relevant degree and sufficient experience who takes responsibility for technical disclosure (e.g. NI 43-101 for mineral projects). QP status links professional accountability to public & investor protection.
