The Four Ethical Theories — mindmap

Mill — Utilitarianism

  • Best act = greatest good for the greatest number
  • Judge by consequences
  • Benefits weighed equally — no favourites

Kant — Duty Ethics (Formalism)

  • Act from duty, by universal rules
  • Intention matters, not results
  • Categorical imperative

Locke — Rights Ethics

  • People have fundamental rights (life, liberty, property)
  • Acts must respect others' rights

Aristotle — Virtue Ethics

  • Act as a person of good character would
  • The "golden mean" — virtue between extremes

How to use them on the exam: a strong ethical answer tests a dilemma against more than one theory — consequences (utilitarian), duties & rules (Kant), rights of those affected (Locke), and what a person of integrity would do (Aristotle). When the theories agree, the answer is clear; when they conflict, the duty to public welfare usually governs.
