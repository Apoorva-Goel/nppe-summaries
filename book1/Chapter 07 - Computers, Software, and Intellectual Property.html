Case History: The Hartford Arena Collapse (1978)

The arena's space-frame roof collapsed under less than half its rated snow load. Cause: the stress-analysis software failed to distinguish compression from tension in the members — and the engineers never validated the computer output with a simple sanity check.

Ethical lesson: relying on software output without verification is a failure of routine due diligence. The professional — not the software — is responsible for the result.

Engineer's takeaway: a computer is a tool, not a substitute for judgment. Always validate software results against independent checks, use licensed software, and treat the output as your professional work product. (IP rights — patents, copyright, trade secrets — protect the software and designs you create.)
