Association's duty to protect the public
▼ splits by who the target is ▼
ENFORCEMENTAgainst UNLICENSED people who practise, or use a title/seal implying a licence → prosecution, fines, contempt
DISCIPLINEAgainst LICENSED members for misconduct → hearing, sanctions up to licence revocation

Example: in 2005 a Toronto man was jailed 30 days + $20,000 costs for contempt after practising without a licence.

Six Grounds for Discipline

Professional misconduct

The most common complaint — "unprofessional conduct." Includes specific acts (e.g. improperly signing/sealing documents).

Incompetence

Lack of knowledge/skill/judgment to a degree that endangers the public.

Negligence

Carelessness falling below the expected standard of care.

Breach of the Code of Ethics

Violating the profession's ethical rules.

Physical/mental incapacity

Inability to practise safely.

Conviction of a serious offence

A criminal conviction bearing on fitness to practise.

NPPE focus: know the enforcement vs discipline distinction cold, and the six grounds. Definitions can't capture every form of misconduct — regulators retain broad discretion to protect the public.
