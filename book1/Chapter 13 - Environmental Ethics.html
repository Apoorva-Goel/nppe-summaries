The Duty to Society — "Reasonable Care"

Know the law

Knowledge of environmental law.

Be competent

Adequate technical knowledge.

Analyze fully

Thorough analysis of impacts.

Hold the line

Insist on high ethical standards.

The Code of Ethics binds professionals to protect public welfare — including the environment. The duty requires reasonable care, not perfection.

Duty to Employer — and Its Limits

You owe your employer loyalty — but not for: (1) illegal actions, (2) actions contrary to the Code of Ethics/environmental guidelines, or (3) actions contrary to your conscience. Whistle-blowing/reporting is rare but may be justified when public/environmental safety is at risk — do it carefully, through proper channels, with documentation. Ethical stands can carry unfair personal consequences.

Key Federal Environmental Laws

CEPA 1999

The main federal environmental statute.

Fisheries Act

Forbids degrading fish habitat.

CEAA 2012 + Enforcement Act

Environmental assessment for sustainable development; strengthens enforcement of 9 federal Acts.

First responsibility: if your work affects the environment, know the law and follow it. The duty to protect the environment can override employer instructions.
