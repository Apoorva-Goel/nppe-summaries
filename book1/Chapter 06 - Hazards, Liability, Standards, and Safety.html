Contract law

Discipline

  • By the Licensing Association
  • For negligence/incompetence

The 4 Elements of a Negligence Tort — all required

1 · Duty of care owed
2 · That duty breached
3 · Plaintiff suffered loss
4 · Breach was the proximate cause
▼ miss any one → the lawsuit fails ▼
Liability in tort

Duty of Care & Safety Practice

When a duty exists

When a reasonably foreseeable risk of injury exists and someone is close enough to be affected. Not owed to everyone — only likely victims.

Safe design

Follow design codes, standards & best practices; know safety regulations; do formal hazard analyses.

Workplace safety

Learn & follow your province's Occupational Health & Safety law on every site.

Engineer's takeaway: one negligent act can trigger contract, tort, AND disciplinary consequences at once. The best defence is standards-based design, documented hazard analysis, and strict OH&S compliance.
