The National Pollutant Release Inventory (NPRI)

Canada's legislated, publicly accessible inventory of pollutant releases to air, water & land (plus disposals & transfers). It lists 300+ pollutants in six categories — the Core list alone has ~195 substances. About 7,280 facilities reported in 2015. Reporting is triggered when quantities meet set criteria.

Why NPRI exists: pollutant registers worldwide were created after the Bhopal, India industrial disaster — the deadliest industrial accident in history. Transparency is the first line of public protection.

Environmental Indicators — how we measure health

Air & climate

Greenhouse-gas emissions, ambient air pollutants, national air-pollutant emissions.

Water

Freshwater quality in rivers, water availability, restoring Great Lakes areas of concern.

Nature

Protected areas, ecological integrity of national parks, status of major fish stocks.

Engineer/geoscientist takeaway: if your facility emits regulated substances, you likely have NPRI reporting obligations. Industrial activity discharges millions of tonnes of pollutants yearly — measurement & disclosure are the foundation of regulation and public trust.
