Three Client-Consultant Models — a spectrum

IndependentConsultant decides & acts with little client input
BalancedConsultant advises & weighs options with the client — usually best
AgentConsultant acts on the client's instructions
Best practice: agree the working relationship at the start — usually near the middle ("balanced") of the spectrum, so the client stays informed while the consultant applies professional judgment.

Advertising — the rules

Clear & factual

No exaggeration; in good taste; must not diminish the dignity/image of the profession.

No disparagement

Must not criticize or claim superiority over other professionals; comply with all laws.

Supervision & fees

Advertise only services under a member's direct supervision. Fee references vary by province (Alberta restricts; Quebec/Ontario permit factual fee info under conditions).

NPPE focus: consultants may advertise, but it must be professional, factual, dignified, and non-disparaging. Rules on mentioning fees differ by province — know your Association's code & practice guidelines.
