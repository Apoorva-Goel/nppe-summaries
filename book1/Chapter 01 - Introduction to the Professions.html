The Two Professions

Engineering

Applies science & math to design and build for the use and convenience of people — bridges, structures, machines, systems. A profession respected alongside law & medicine.

Geoscience

Studies the Earth — discovering, analyzing & developing resources. Canada's Geological Survey (now part of NRCan) dates to Sir William Logan (Mount Logan bears his name).

An Inspiring Legacy

Rideau Canal (1832)

Canada's first megaproject — 202 km of dams, locks & waterways by Lt-Col John By. A UNESCO World Heritage Site (2007).

"Top Ten" achievements

A 1987 EIC jury named Canada's top engineering & geoscience feats — from 110+ nominations.

Geological Survey

Founded 1842 (£1,500 grant) — Canada's principal agency for geoscientific research.

Learning from failure: the Quebec Bridge collapses (1907, 1916) killed dozens and became a defining lesson in engineering responsibility — later memorialized in the Iron Ring ceremony. Failures shape professional duty as much as successes do.
The throughline: both professions exist to serve and protect the public — the value that justifies their special status and the regulation that follows (Ch 2).
