Bonds

A surety's guarantee that the principal will perform — similar to, but not the same as, insurance.

The Three Roles

Surety

Guarantees the principal's performance for the obligee's benefit. Paid a premium.

Principal

The contractor whose performance is guaranteed. Indemnifies the surety.

Obligee

The owner — protected if the principal defaults.

Two separate agreements are involved, each with its own obligations. Suretyship contracts must be in writing. A surety can be released if the obligee's acts prejudice it (e.g. material changes without consent).

Surety's Recourse & Bond Types

After paying out

The surety is subrogated to the obligee's rights and usually holds indemnities from the contractor & shareholders — though both may be worthless if the contractor is insolvent.

Bid bond

Almost exclusively in construction — typically 10% of the bid. Guarantees the bidder will enter the contract (and provide further bonds) if selected.

Bond vs insurance: like Book 2 Ch 26 — a bond is not insurance. The surety expects to be reimbursed by the principal, so the contractor remains ultimately responsible.
