Disclosure & Technical Reporting

The core obligation

Corporations must disclose adequate & timely material information so investors can make informed decisions — plus ongoing disclosure of material changes.

Technical standards (post BRE-X)

After the BRE-X fraud, the CSA adopted NI 43-101 (mining) and NI 51-101 (oil & gas) to standardize disclosure of scientific/technical information.

The "Qualified Person": technical disclosure must be prepared/supervised by a QP following CSA guidelines. Responsibility for choosing the QP and proper use of their information rests with the company's officers and directors.

Liability for Professionals

Misrepresentation / negligence

Geoscientists & engineers face liability for misrepresentation or negligence in technical reports — and securities law adds a statutory cause of action where reports support a prospectus.

Insider trading (Criminal Code s.382.1)

Prohibited to trade — or tip others — on confidential, material information you know (or should know) is confidential.

Geoscientist's takeaway: if your technical report feeds a public disclosure, it carries securities-law liability. Follow NI 43-101/51-101, act as a diligent Qualified Person, and never trade or tip on non-public results (remember BRE-X).
