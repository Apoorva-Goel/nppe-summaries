Labour Law

The law of union–management relations — distinct from individual employment law (Ch 21).
Industrial peaceCharter s.2(d)Sole bargaining agentEmployee categories

Foundations

Purpose

Promote industrial peace and balance the rights of employers and organized employees. Unions arose in response to oppressive conditions.

Constitutional right

The right to form a union is protected by Charter s.2(d) — freedom of association.

Sole bargaining agent: labour legislation designates the union as the exclusive representative of the employees in a bargaining unit for negotiating the collective agreement.

Three Worker Categories — the line matters

Employee

Works under the employer's control — full labour/employment protections.

Dependent contractor

A statutory middle category — economically reliant on one payer ("in the labour market, not the product market"); often gets union protections.

Independent contractor

Truly in business for themselves — outside most labour protections.

Engineer/manager takeaway: the employee vs contractor distinction "is not always obvious" and has big consequences (union rights, liability, taxes). The dependent contractor category catches workers who look independent but depend on a single payer.
