Implied Terms in Every Employment Contract

Employee — fidelity

A duty of loyalty; employees are agents of the employer. Includes protecting confidential information.

Employee — competence

A duty to perform the job with reasonable competence.

Employer — notice

Duty to give adequate notice of termination, or pay in lieu (salary + benefits for the notice period).

These apply unless express terms say otherwise. The employment contract may be written, oral, or partly both.

Trade Secrets & Confidential Information

Confidential information is protected where it: is used in an industry, is not widely known, has value because it isn't known, and is kept secret through reasonable efforts. Trade secrets needn't be exotic formulas — customer lists and methods can qualify. They let companies compete; misuse by a departing employee is actionable.

Engineer's takeaway: termination turns on reasonable notice (notice/pay-in-lieu) — often longer than statutory minimums. As an employee you owe loyalty and must protect your employer's confidential information; as a manager, use clear written terms and confidentiality clauses.
