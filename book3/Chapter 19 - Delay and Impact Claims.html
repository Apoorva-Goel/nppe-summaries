Delay & Impact Claims

Delays cost money — who pays depends on the schedule, the critical path, and the contract wording.

Scheduling Tools

Bar (Gantt) chart

Shows activities over time — simple, but doesn't show dependencies.

Network diagram (CPM)

Links activities so dependencies and the critical path are visible.

Float

The time contingency in an activity — activities with zero float are on the critical path.

Types of Delay

Compensable

Caused by the other party (e.g. owner/consultant act or omission) → entitled to damages + time.

Excusable

Beyond the contractor's control but not the owner's fault → usually a time extension, no money.

Force majeure

"Acts of God"/uncontrollable events → handled per the force-majeure clause.

Two decisive details: (1) most contracts require the delay to hit a critical-path activity to earn an extension, and (2) notice provisions are strict — miss the notice and you can lose an otherwise valid claim. Concurrent delays (both parties at fault) complicate entitlement.
