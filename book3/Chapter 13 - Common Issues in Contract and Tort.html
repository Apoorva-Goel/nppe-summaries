Concurrent Liability — Central Trust v Rafuse

The SCC held that parties to a contract can also owe a duty of care in tort. A plaintiff may pursue both. The practical lesson: define your obligations as precisely as possible in the contract, since the tort duty runs alongside it.

Limitation Periods

Discoverability

The clock often starts when the claim was or ought to have been discovered. Outcome can turn on whether a defect is latent (hidden) or patent (obvious).

Ultimate limitation

A hard backstop (e.g. 10 years) beyond which claims can't be brought — even for latent defects.

Three Protections for Professionals

Contractual time limit

Agree (where the statute allows) to a shorter period — e.g. liability ceases 6 years after substantial performance.

Liability caps

Cap claims at a dollar figure, available insurance, re-performance of the services, or a combination.

Insurance for life

Because liability can surface late, professional liability insurance must generally be maintained throughout one's lifetime.

Engineer's takeaway: negotiate limitation-of-liability clauses into your agreements (time + dollar caps), understand your province's ultimate limitation period, and keep PLI in force long after a project ends — latent defects can emerge years later.
