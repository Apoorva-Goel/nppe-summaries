Insurance

Contracts of indemnity that spread and shift risk — with their own special rules.
Contract of indemnityDuty to defendSubrogationInsurable interest

Four Key Concepts — mindmap

Contract of indemnity

  • Ordinary contract rules apply (offer, acceptance, consideration, mistake)
  • Spreads & shifts risk

Duty to defend

  • Insurer must indemnify AND defend
  • No duty if claim is only for excluded conduct (e.g. fraud)

Subrogation

  • Insurer "steps into the shoes" of the insured
  • Recovers from third parties

Insurable interest

  • Insured must benefit from / be prejudiced by the loss
  • Required for a valid policy

Operating Without Insurance

The danger

A single large claim can cause bankruptcy. Not recommended for anyone "with anything to lose."

"Judgment proof" tactics

Some try to put assets beyond creditors (e.g. transfers, thinly-capitalized corporations) — risky and limited.

Wrap-up tip: naming all project participants as insureds on one policy (a "wrap-up" / OCIP) eliminates subrogation claims between them — they can't sue each other through the insurer. Always confirm coverage scope and exclusions.
