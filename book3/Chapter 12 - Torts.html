Where Negligence Comes From

Donoghue v Stevenson created the modern "neighbour principle": you must take reasonable care to avoid acts or omissions you can reasonably foresee would likely injure your neighbour. A duty can exist even with no contract between the parties.

Negligence established

Torts split into unintentional (negligence) and intentional (e.g. trespass, nuisance, defamation).

Duty of Care for Architects & Engineers

To owners

Designers owe a duty to protect owners even without a contract between them.

To bidders — Edgeworth

A designer can owe a duty to a successful bidder for negligent plans/specs. (But the claim against the individual engineers who sealed the plans was dismissed.)

To workers

A designer may owe workers a duty re: unsafe practices — but workers' comp bars many such negligence claims.

Engineer's takeaway: tort duties reach beyond your contract — to owners, bidders, even third parties. A negligent contractor generally can't offload blame onto the designer for failing to catch the contractor's own mistakes.
