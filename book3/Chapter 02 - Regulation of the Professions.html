Two Pillars of Self-Regulation

Right to title

Only licensed members may use the profession's title (e.g. "P.Eng.", "Architect", "P.Geo."). Protecting the title is a core duty of the provincial regulator.

Scope of practice

The professions have an exclusive scope — defined in each province's statute. Practising within it generally requires a licence.

Definition of "practice of professional engineering"

Any act of planning, designing, composing, evaluating, advising, reporting, directing, supervising or managing that requires application of engineering principles and concerns the safeguarding of life, health, property, economic interests, public welfare or the environment. (Note: definitions are somewhat "circular" — keyed to the application of professional principles.)

NPPE focus: regulation exists to protect the public. Know the difference between title protection and scope-of-practice protection, and that statutes include exceptions/exclusions (e.g. holders of certain science degrees doing non-reserved work).
