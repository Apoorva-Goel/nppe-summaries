Ethics & the Law — three kinds of consequence

Criminal

Prosecuted by the state; standard = beyond a reasonable doubt. Criminal conduct in one's work is almost always also unethical.

Civil liability

e.g. negligence; standard = balance of probabilities. Often also triggers discipline.

Disciplinary

By the regulator — for professional misconduct/incompetence. One act can trigger all three at once.

Key ideas: ethics deals in "shades of grey" — codes guide the right course. Conflicts of interest are the most common ethical trap: disclose them. If accused of an ethics breach, get legal advice early. The duty to the public is always paramount.
