Classification of Property — mindmap

  • Land
  • Buildings & fixtures attached to benefit the land

Tangible Personal

  • "Chattels"
  • Movable physical things

Intangible Personal

  • Rights without physical form
  • incl. intellectual property

Why it matters

  • Different laws govern each
  • Fundamental distinction

Rights in Real Property

Fee simple

The greatest right — closest to "ownership." Can be held by more than one party (e.g. joint tenants with right of survivorship).

Subsurface & air

Governments usually reserve minerals, oil & gas. Air rights extend a reasonable distance above the land.

Boundaries & support

Every parcel has boundaries, a right to lateral support, and special riparian rights where water is involved.

"Runs with the land": some rights (e.g. easements, restrictive covenants) bind future owners, not just the original parties — critical when a project sits on or near someone else's interest. Always confirm who holds subsurface and air rights.
