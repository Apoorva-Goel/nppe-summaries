The Four Forms — mindmap

Sole Proprietorship

  • One individual / trade name
  • Simple to set up
  • Unlimited personal liability

Partnership

  • Governed by Partnership Act
  • Each partner can bind the firm
  • Personal liability + fiduciary duty

Corporation

  • Separate legal person
  • Limited liability
  • Federal (CBCA) or provincial

Joint Venture

  • Parties combine for one project
  • Contractual, not a standing entity

Partnership Variations

General partnership

All partners share management and unlimited liability for the firm's debts.

Limited partnership

≥1 general + ≥1 limited partner. Limited partners can't manage or they lose limited-liability protection.

LLP

Available to professionals in some provinces — shields a partner from others' negligence.

Professional caveat: provincial statutes restrict how corporations may practise engineering/architecture/geoscience (e.g. a Certificate of Authorization). And some statutes pierce limited liability, placing personal responsibility on directors. Partners owe each other a fiduciary duty of trust.
