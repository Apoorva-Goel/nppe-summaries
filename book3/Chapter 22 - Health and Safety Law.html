The Three Fundamental Worker Rights

Right to KNOW

About hazards in the workplace (e.g. WHMIS, training, labelling).

Right to PARTICIPATE

In health & safety — e.g. joint health & safety committees.

Right to REFUSE

Unsafe work without reprisal.

OH&S is a mix of common law + federal/provincial legislation, enforced through site visits/inspections. (This chapter = workplace safety, not design safety.)

Criminal Liability — s.217.1 (Bill C-45, 2004)

After the Westray mine disaster, the Criminal Code was amended: anyone who directs how work is done has a legal duty to take reasonable steps to protect workers and the public. Breach can mean criminal negligence causing bodily harm or death — for individuals AND organizations.

First conviction: Transpavé Inc. (Quebec, 2008) — the first organization convicted of criminal negligence under these provisions.

Engineer/supervisor takeaway: if you direct work, you carry a personal legal duty for safety. Failing it isn't just a regulatory fine — it can be a criminal offence for you and your company. Document hazard controls and respect the right to refuse.
