Federal Law — PIPEDA

The Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act governs how organizations handle personal information. It does not apply to domestic, journalistic, artistic, or literary collection, and it includes a complaints process.

Core PIPEDA Obligations — mindmap

Purpose & consent

  • Identify why you collect
  • Get consent first

Minimize

  • Collect only what's required
  • Use only for that purpose

Protect

  • Keep it accurate
  • Adequate safeguards

Access

  • Individuals can access their info
  • And the privacy policy

Provincial Legislation — Two Models

Privacy as a tort

Creates a private right to sue for violation (e.g. Saskatchewan's Privacy Act — a wilful violation is "actionable without proof of damage").

Quasi-criminal offence

Treats breaches as offences, sometimes with serious penalties.

Engineer/firm takeaway: if your organization collects personal data (clients, employees, the public), PIPEDA's consent + purpose + safeguard + access rules apply — and some provinces let individuals sue directly for breaches. Have a privacy policy and protect the data you hold.
