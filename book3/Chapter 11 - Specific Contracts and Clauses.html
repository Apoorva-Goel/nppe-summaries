Specific Contracts & Clauses

In construction, the contract type is usually defined by how the contractor gets paid.

Standard Form Contracts

Industry groups (CCDC, AIA, etc.) publish standard forms balancing both sides' interests. Biggest pitfall: they get used without tailoring to the specific deal. Always fit the form to the project.

Payment Methods — how the contractor is paid

Fixed price / stipulated sum

One lump sum incl. overhead & profit. Myth: "always best for the owner." Reality: a fixed price rarely stays fixed — treat it as a starting point. Most common form: CCDC 2.

Cost-plus

Contractor paid actual cost + a fee. Owner risk: profit inflation via transfer pricing, and weaker cost control. Useful when scope is uncertain.

Other Contract Families

Geoscience forms

Syndication, grubstake, royalty, option agreements — special to mineral exploration.

Professional service agreements

Used to retain a professional or firm for a project.

Financing parties

Banks & investors bring their own loan/security agreements into the project.

Engineer's takeaway: know what each payment model does to risk — fixed price loads cost risk on the contractor (who prices it in); cost-plus loads it on the owner. Never sign a standard form without reading the supplementary conditions.
