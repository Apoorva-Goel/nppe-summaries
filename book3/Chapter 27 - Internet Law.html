Internet Law

No single "internet law" — existing legal principles applied to a borderless medium.

Existing Law, New Medium — mindmap

Jurisdiction

  • "Conflict of laws"
  • Which court / whose law?
  • Hard — no borders

Torts

  • Defamation (libel/slander) online
  • Negligent advice → misrepresentation

Copyright

  • Easy high-quality copying
  • Recourse = the courts

Trademarks

  • Domain names subject to trademark law
  • Like corporate names

Privacy & securities

  • PIPEDA applies online (Ch 26)
  • Securities rules apply too (Ch 25)
Engineer's takeaway: the internet didn't get its own legal code — courts apply existing tort, contract, copyright, trademark, privacy & securities law to it. The hardest question is usually jurisdiction: a borderless medium makes "whose law applies?" genuinely difficult. Watch what your firm posts and emails — defamation and negligent-advice claims follow online.
