How Law Is Created

Statutes & regulations

Legislatures enact statutes; they often delegate power to agencies to make regulations that also have force of law.

Common law

Large bodies of law are judge-made through precedent. Almost all of Canada (and the US) is a common-law jurisdiction.

Quebec is the exception: it uses a civil law system built on a codified Civil Code, not common-law precedent. The law is "not static" — it evolves, which makes it imprecise and somewhat unpredictable.
